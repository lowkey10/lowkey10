Gender based violence is a term used to define harm inflicted to an individual or a group of people mostly women and girls.It can be directed at someone based on their sex and social norms.Gender based violence comes in various forms but all in, it is gender bias and inflicts the idea of gender inequality at the core of everyone's mind hence why it violates human rights within societies. Regardless of how bitter its outcomes are, the whole world is tryng at its best to fight and prevent it through ways that could curb it totally or at least cut down its virulent repercussions. It's the little things that give rise to gender based violence such as cultural norms which permit men to discipline women by physical means, this leaves females vulnerable since it often leads to sexual abuse as they are less powerful for society has labelled women inferior to men in many aspects of life. Girls and young women regularly experience abuse at home from physical punishment to sexual, emotional or psychological violence. According to the WHO one-third of women world wide have gone through either physical or sexual abuse. The prevelance is lowest in developed states with 23% and highest in African countries as well as Southern Asian countries with Africa owning up tp 46% and Asia 40% respectively. Not only does it negatively affect one mentally but also physically.
